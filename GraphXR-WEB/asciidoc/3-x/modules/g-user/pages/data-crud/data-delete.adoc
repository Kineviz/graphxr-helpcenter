= Delete Data

You can delete data at any time:
 
* Delete selected nodes and edges from the canvas.
* Delete a property and its values from the nodes (or edges) of a category (or relationship).
* Clear all data from the project, including any hidden data.
* Clean unused category and relationship labels from the *_Project_* panel's *_Category_* and *_Relationship_* subpanels.

Data is deleted from the project canvas, not from saved views or the connected internal or external database. 

== Delete Selected Nodes 

*To delete a data selection:*

. Select data using any method.
. Select *_Delete_* (trash can icon) on the toolbar or the right-click menu (or press *_del_* or *_backspace_*).
+
image::/v3/05_20_01_ToolbarDeleteIcon.png[,720,340,role=text-left]

TIP: Use *_Ctrl_*+*_Z_* to immediately undo a *_Delete_* or *_Clear_* action.

== Clear All Data

*To clear all data from a project:*

* Click the *_Clear_* toolbar icon (*_Ctrl_* + *_Shift_* + *_C_*).
+
image::/v3/05_20_02_ToolbarClearIcon.png[,720,340,role=text-left]

TIP: Use *_Ctrl_*+*_Z_* to immediately undo a *_Delete_* or *_Clear_* action.

== Delete a Property

In an Enhanced Table you can delete any property and its values from all the nodes (or edges) of a category (or relationship).

*To delete a property:*

. Deselect all the data on the canvas.
. Open the *_Table_* panel, and  the *_Category_* or *_Relationship_* subpanel.
. Click to select the category (or relationship) that includes the property you want to delete.

+
image::/v3/05_19_01_PropDeleteTable.png[,520,340,role=text-left]
+

. Click *_Enhanced Table_* to open the editable table.
. Click the settings icon next to the property name, and select *_Delete_*.

+
image::/v3/05_19_02_PropDeleteEnhanced.png[,520,340,role=text-left]
+

+
The property column is removed.
+

+
image::/v3/05_19_03_PropDeleted.png[,520,340,role=text-left]
+

. Click the *X* in the upper right corner to close the *_Enhanced Table_*.

+ 
The property and its values are now deleted from the basic table, and no longer appear in information panels, style setting dialogs, or the property lists in the *_Projects_* panel.

+
image::/v3/05_19_04_PropDeletedBasicTable.png[,520,340,role=text-left]


== Clean Unused Labels

When you delete data or clear the graph, the data model and its metadata persists. This means that the *_Categories_* and *_Relationships_* subpanels in the *_Project_* panel retain category and relationship labels even when no entities of that type remain in the project.

To clean out unused labels you can: 
 
* Use the *_Clean_* button that appears when a category or relationship is unused, or
* Use the *_Clean Unused Config for Categories/Relationships_* button in the *_Settings_* panel to remove all unused labels in one step. 

=== Clean an unused label

*To remove a single unused entity label:*

. Open the *_Project_* panel and *_Category_* or *_Relationship_* subpanel, and click an unused label.
+
image::/v3/05_20_03_CleanSingle.png[,520,220,role=text-left]
+
. Click its *_Clean_* button.

+
A *_Warning_* message asks you to confirm removal of the metadata for the entity.

+
image::/v3/05_20_04_CleanWarning.png[,520,220,role=text-left]
+
. Click *_OK_* to confirm. 
+
The unused label disappears from the *_Category_* or *_Relationship_* subpanel.
   
=== Clean all unused labels

*To clean all unused entity labels:*

. Open the *_Project_* panel and *_Settings_* subpanel, and click the *_Advanced Settings_* dropdown arrow.
. Scroll to the bottom of the panel, and click *_Clean Unused Config for Categories/Relationships_*.
+
image::/v3/05_20_05_CleanAllUnused.png[,520,220,role=text-left]
+

+
A message indicates successful removal of the unused labels.
+

. Re-open the *_Category_* and *_Relationship_* panels to see that the only remaining labels are for nodes and edges currently on the canvas. 

== Empty or delete a project's internal database

In some situations, you may want to empty a project's internal database, for example, if it includes transformed graph elements you no longer find useful. 

You can also delete a project's connected internal database – a more extreme action. You might do this for a project where you want the saved views to persist, but not the data in the internal database.   

IMPORTANT: Consider exporting the current database before emptying or deleting it. 

NOTE: To manage the internal database, the project must be configured to grant access to the *_Project > Data Store_* subpanel. 

*To empty the internal connected database:*

. Open the *_Project > Data Store_* subpanel.
. In the *_Data Store_* section, optionally click *_Export Database_* 
. Click *_Empty Database_*
+
Confirmation message
+
. Click  *_Empty Database_* to proceed, or *_Cancel_* to exit.
 
*To delete the internal connected database:*

. Open the *_Project > Data Store_* subpanel.
. In the *_Data Store_* section, optionally click *_Export Database_* 
. Click the dropdown arrow to open the *_Danger Zone_*.
. Click *_Delete Database_*.
+
Confirmation message
+
. Click  *_Delete_* to proceed, or *_Cancel_* to exit.
